# International Trade Analysis: Spain and Japan (2013-2024)

## Project Overview
This project provides a comprehensive empirical analysis of the trade dynamics between **Spain** and **Japan** over a decade. It was developed as a **COIL (Collaborative Online International Learning) Project** in collaboration between **Özyeğin University** and the **University of Dayton**.

The study examines how trade flows have evolved, specifically focusing on the impact of the **EU-Japan Economic Partnership Agreement (EPA)** that took effect in 2019, and evaluates sectoral strengths through various economic lenses.

## Key Research Areas
- **Theoretical Framework:** Application of Ricardian and Heckscher-Ohlin models to explain trade patterns.
- **Policy Impact:** Statistical analysis (t-test) of the pre- and post-2019 EPA period.
- **Intra-Industry Trade:** Calculation of the **Grubel-Lloyd Index** to measure industry-specific trade integration.
- **Comparative Advantage:** Identification of sectors where Spain holds a competitive edge (Agri-food, Chemicals, and Industrial Goods).

## Repository Structure
- 📁 **Data:** `coilLASTdataset.xlsx` — The primary dataset containing trade volumes and sectoral descriptions.
- 📁 **Scripts:** `CoilRscript.R` — Comprehensive R script used for data cleaning, visualization (ggplot2), and statistical testing.
- 📁 **Presentation:** `International Trade_ Japan and Spain (3).pdf` — The final presentation covering economic theories and geopolitical analysis.

## Methodology & Tools
- **Language:** R
- **Key Libraries:** `tidyverse`, `ggplot2`, `dplyr`, `corrplot`, `plotly`.
- **Statistical Methods:** Independent Two-Sample T-Test, Time Series Visualization, and Correlation Matrices.

## Key Findings
- **EPA Impact:** The 2019 agreement led to a statistically significant increase in Spanish exports to Japan (p-value: 0.0014).
- **Resilience:** Despite the COVID-19 shock in 2020, trade volumes showed high recovery speed, emphasizing strong bilateral economic ties.
- **Sectoral Trends:** Spain maintains a consistent trade surplus in land/labor-intensive sectors (Food & Beverages), while Japan leads in capital-intensive high-tech manufacturing.
